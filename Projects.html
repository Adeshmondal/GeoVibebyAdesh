Feature Importance

Feature importance quantifies the influence of each feature in predicting the target variable

It helps to identify which features have the most impact on the model’s predictions and can guide in feature selection, improving model performance, and understanding the data better.

In Random Forest regression, feature importance is typically determined using one of the following methods: This method calculates the importance of a feature by looking at how much the feature decreases the impurity in the data when it is used in a split.

Conclusion

The performance metrics of the Random Forest model, including RMSE, R², MSE, and MAE, indicate a strong predictive capability for Aman rice yield using remote sensing-derived variables. The high R² values across multiple years (ranging from 0.80 to 0.97) strongly suggest that there is a significant relationship between the selected vegetation indices and VV band and Aman rice yield.

The study successfully identified key remote sensing-derived variables, such as the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) and RGVI, which are crucial for predicting Aman rice yield. These variables were found to have a significant influence on the yield, as demonstrated by the model’s high R² values and low RMSE and MAE values.

Indices factors, such as NDVI, MSI, RGVI, LAI, LSWI, NDWI, SAVI, EVI and VV were integrated with the remote sensing data to improve the model's accuracy, indicating their importance in yield prediction.

The Random Forest model demonstrated robust performance in predicting Aman rice yield across different years. The model achieved high R² values, indicating a strong fit between the predicted and actual yields. The model's average RMSE, MSE, and MAE values were reasonably low, suggesting that the predictions were accurate and reliable.

Implications

The study successfully developed and evaluated a machine learning model for predicting Aman rice yield using multi-sensor remote sensing datasets. The findings indicate a strong relationship between remote sensing-derived variables, such as NDVI and RGVI index, and Aman rice yield.

The Random Forest model proved to be effective in yield prediction, with high accuracy and reliability. These results have significant implications for agricultural planning and management in the Kangsabati River Basin, providing a valuable tool for farmers and policymakers to optimize rice production and ensure food security.
